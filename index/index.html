Journal Article

Climate Change Impact on Coastal Communities: A Case Study of Bangladesh

Rumana Islam Rupa, Ahmed K., Johnson M.
Journal of Environmental Studies
2023
DOI: 10.1234/jes.2023.001

This study examines the impact of climate change on coastal communities in Bangladesh, focusing on adaptation strategies and resilience building. Through a mixed-methods approach involving surveys, interviews, and participatory workshops, we identified key vulnerabilities and community-based solutions. Results indicate that local knowledge systems play a crucial role in developing effective adaptation strategies. The study proposes a framework for integrating indigenous knowledge with scientific approaches to enhance community resilience against climate-induced challenges.

Conference Paper

Sustainable Development Practices in Higher Education: Challenges and Opportunities

Rahman S., Rumana Islam Rupa
International Conference on Education for Sustainable Development
2022
Bangkok, Thailand

This paper explores the integration of sustainable development practices in higher education institutions, with a particular focus on universities in developing countries. We present findings from a comparative study of sustainability initiatives across ten universities in South Asia. The research highlights significant barriers to implementation, including resource constraints, policy gaps, and institutional resistance. We propose a comprehensive framework for embedding sustainability in curriculum, research, operations, and community engagement. The paper concludes with practical recommendations for university administrators and policymakers.

Journal Article

Gender Disparities in STEM Education: A Longitudinal Study of Undergraduate Students

Rumana Islam Rupa, Chen L., Patel R.
International Journal of Gender Studies in Education
2021
DOI: 10.5678/ijgse.2021.045

This longitudinal study investigates gender disparities in STEM education among undergraduate students over a five-year period. Using mixed methods research, we tracked 250 students from enrollment to graduation, examining factors influencing persistence and achievement. Our findings reveal complex interactions between institutional culture, pedagogical approaches, and gender-based stereotypes that contribute to differential outcomes. The study identifies critical intervention points and effective strategies for promoting gender equity in STEM fields. Recommendations include curriculum reforms, mentorship programs, and institutional policy changes to create more inclusive learning environments.
